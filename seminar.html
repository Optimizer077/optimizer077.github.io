Reasoning Models and Image Restoration

Large language models, multimodal agents, and step-wise restoration strategies.

This talk covered how reasoning-oriented language models and vision-language systems can support difficult restoration tasks, especially when the pipeline benefits from explicit decomposition and agent-style planning.

Referenced Papers

  • Improving Language Model Reasoning with Self-Motivated Learning
  • RestoreAgent: Autonomous Image Restoration Agent via Multimodal LLMs
  • Chain-of-Restoration: Universal Step-by-Step Restorers

Multi-Task Learning Perspective for Diffusion Models

Transferability, negative transfer, and mixture-of-experts views of denoising.

This session framed diffusion models through the lens of multi-task learning and discussed when shared representations help, when they interfere, and how routing strategies can improve overall robustness.

Referenced Papers

  • Addressing Negative Transfer in Diffusion Models
  • Switch Diffusion Transformer

Task-Oriented Diffusion for Image Restoration

Restoration-first diffusion systems tailored for practical image quality tasks.

A focused deep dive into diffusion-based restoration models including Ambient Diffusion, ResShift, and DiffBIR, with attention to how each system balances visual quality, speed, and task specificity.

Referenced Papers

  • Ambient Diffusion
  • ResShift: Efficient SR via Residual Shifting
  • DiffBIR: Diffusion-based Blind Image Restoration

Any-Size Diffusion and ResDiff

Highlights from two AAAI 2024 papers on synthesis and super-resolution.

This seminar compared Any-Size-Diffusion for arbitrary-resolution text-to-image generation and ResDiff for single-image super-resolution, focusing on how each paper combines structure with generation quality.

Referenced Papers

  • Any-Size-Diffusion: Text-Driven Synthesis for Any-Size HD Images
  • ResDiff: Combining CNN and Diffusion for Image SR

NeurIPS 2024 Paper Summaries

A summary session focused on notable conference papers and their broader implications.

This closing session gathered key NeurIPS 2024 papers into a compact overview, helping connect individual contributions to larger trends across the field.
